What is Crop Rotation?

Crop rotation is a sustainable farming practice where different crops are planted in the same area in sequential seasons to improve soil fertility, reduce pest and disease risks, and increase crop yield.

By rotating crops, farmers can naturally manage soil health and fertility, avoid the build-up of pests and diseases, and use resources more efficiently. Crop rotation is a cornerstone of regenerative agriculture, promoting biodiversity and ecological balance.
